The public Augmented Reality kinectome, or pARk, is a framework for 5G immersive internets.

In a pARk, you can interact with the world as you are meant to -- with your hands and natural senses -- while still possessing the benefits of digital impressions, search, and machine processing.

We are no longer constrained to abstractions like the Document Object Model. Instead, make digital services and experiences part-and-parcel of the natural flow of your every day life.

Each network is managed by a distributed autonomous organization, or DAO, known as a Community that exposes pARk access via the I See Dead People paradigm -- also known as ICDP. There are three (3) components to an ICDP system:

  1. Immersive Internet of Things or I2oT
  2. Community as a Service or CaaS
  3. Distributed Ledger Technology or DLT

The Immersive Internet of Things is a subset of the more widely-known Internet of Things (IoT) concept. I2oT devices, just like IoT technologies, can communicate directly with each other, exchanging information from item to item without a centralized, third-party service. Additionally, I2oT devices possess interactive-display capabilities to overlay digital content in real world spaces (webXR).

Community-as-a-Service (CaaS) is the organizational structure of pARk DAOs. Under the CaaS model, pARk networks act as decentralized content exchanges between Community Members (CMs). Every account that posts content to a pARk is rewarded for all the public content they produce -- comments and reactions to other posts included! The network does not differentiate between "users" or "producers", internally every agent is a CM.

Content uploads are transactions in the pARk. All content has an associated Lyokoin -- and consequently US Dollar -- value. The owner of the content owns that value and can use it in other Community transactions on the pARk or exchange Lyokoin for USD to take that value into other markets.

At the core of each pARk is a treeHouse Distributed Ledger (tHDL) that manages, validates, and records pARk data. Uploading content to a pARk initiates an experience generation request. This request contains the experience blueprint, i.e. the content itself, along with the Lyokoin necessary to generate the experience. The tHDL returns a pointer to the decentralized immersive experience, its associated value, and a public record of successful validation.

All of the content in a pARk is wrapped in an experience object so that anyone can interact with the content by itself as a self-contained immersive experience. Internally, these experience objects are linked to or included in other experience objects to form pARk experiences, services, tools, etc.

The hyper-real space of a decentralized immersive application (DIA) found in the pARk is the geographic spread or reach of said application defined as a set of boundaries. Hyper-real spaces are to the pARk what websites are to the World Wide Web (the Web).

Hyper-real spaces are also referred to as ledgerspaces because they are programmatically and digitally represented by blocks of code and hashes recorded in the tHDL. The result of this data structure is that media on the pARk is content-addressed rather insetad of location-addressed like it is on the Web.

Thanks to this fundamental shift in the nature of dat reprsentation, pARk networks form a hyper-reality of chained experiences rather than a simple linked mesh of hyper-text domains. For this reason, pARk hyper-real spaces can be supersets, dimension-wise, of WWW hypertext sites or any mesh networks of a similar dimension-space.

Lyoko is the programming language for pARk smart contracts, or Deeds. Each deed is its own self-contained webXR experience. Inspired by natural language and built with ports to ECMAScript and C++/WebAssembly, members use Lyoko to define content, services, tools, etc.
