Welcome

The public Augmented Reality kinectome, or pARk, is a 5G immersive internet framework.

pARks augment the flow of creative collaboration through technologies and communities that combine the best of the digital and physical realms. In a pARk, one can interact with the world as one is meant to -- without the constraints of abstractions like the Document Object Model -- while still taking advantage of the benefits of digital impressions, search, and machine processing.

pARks are operated by distributed autonomous organizations, or DAOs, known as Communities. Each Community Network operates on an immersive computing paradigm called ICDP (I2oT-CaaS-DLT Paradigm) or "I See Dead People". As the parenthetical denotes, there are three (3) components to this system:

  1. Immersive Internet of Things or I2oT
  2. Community as a Service or CaaS
  3. Distributed Ledger Technology or DLT

The Immersive Internet of Things is a sub-discipline of the more widely-known Internet of Things (IoT) concept. I2oT devices, just like IoT technologies, can communicate directly with each other, exchanging information from item to item without a centralized, third-party service. Additionally, I2oT devices possess interactive-display capabilities to overlay or present digital content in real world spaces.

Community as a Service is the organizational structure of pARk DAOs. Under the CaaS model, pARk networks act as decentralized content exchanges between Community Members (CMs). Because every account that posts on a pARk is rewarded for all the public content they produce -- comments and reactions to other posts included! -- everyone is a CM with respect their network instead of a "user" or "producer".

Internally all content on a pARk is the result of a transaction, an experience generation request with an associated Lyokoin -- and consequently US Dollar -- value. The owner of the content owns that value and can use it in other Community transactions or exchange their Lyokoin for USD and take that value into other markets.

The House of Venus TreeHouse Distributed Ledger Technology manages pARk data layers. Adding content to a pARk occurs via a generation request. Community Members (CMs) define an experience and send that blueprint to the Community Network (CN) along with Lyokoin in exchange for a generated experience, a pointer to the said data and its associated value, and a public record of the successful transaction in the TreeHouse Ledger.

Content on the CN ledger is valued based on a combination of storage-cost and market-added value (MAV). Storage-cost is a caluclated combination of the amount of storage space and the hardware resources needed to render or process the content. MAV is any extra value derived from interactions and speculations by other CMs based on the semantics of the content itself.

All of the content in a pARk is wrapped in an experience object and can be interacted with as a self-contained experience. These experience objects are linked to or included in other experience objects forming a hyper-reality of interlinked or chained experiences rather than hyper-text domains.

show and tell

The blueprints that define content on a CN are built with one's body using "Show and Tell" or programmatically with scripts written in "Lyoko".

Show and Tell is a decentralized web application written in Lyoko that lets community members use their hands and voice to build an experience naturally without the need for a mouse or the plethora of action tools one would expect in a standard 3D editor.

lyoko

Lyoko is the programming language for pARk smart contracts. Inspired by natural conversation and built on top of ECMAScript, members use it to define content. Each Lyoko smart contract is its own self-contained webXR experience.
