pARk

The public Augmented Reality kinectome, or pARk, is a framework for 5G immersive internets.

In a pARk, you can interact with the world as you are meant to -- with your hands and natural senses -- while still possessing the benefits of digital impressions, search, and machine processing.

We are no longer constrained to abstractions like the Document Object Model. Instead, make digital services and experiences part-and-parcel of the natural flow of your every day life.

Each network is managed by a distributed autonomous organization, or DAO, known as a Community. Communities act as decentralized service providers and expose pARk access via the I See Dead People paradigm.

The single greatest advantage of the pARk is the connection between natural motions and digital services. To truly augment human ability, pARk infrastructure is such that a community level operation can be mapped to one simple natural motion.

All pARk-level activity fits into three (3) essential categories:

  • Delivery
  • Localization
  • Hyper-Reality

Delivery refers to any pARk request that involves the movement of an OBJECT-SUBJECT.

Examples of Delivery apps include:

  • a service that sends food from one community member to another
  • an experience that moves objects around a room

Localization is any pARk request to find the position of an OBJECT-SUBJECT or VIEWER.

Examples of Localization apps include:

  • a tool for finding items within a grocery store
  • a service for checking bus movements in real-time

Hyper-Reality are pARk requests that generate immersive experiences, i.e. directly filter VIEWERs, EFFECTORs, and/or SELECTORs.

Examples of Hyper-Reality apps include:

  • an immersive museum host
